She brings me porridge every morning.

It is grey and lumpy and tastes faintly of the leather pouch Della's cooks store the grain in. It is objectively terrible. I eat it anyway, because Anya stands over me with her arms crossed and her face set in the particular expression of someone who will not be argued with, and also because she is right. I still need to eat. I still need to sleep. I still need to do all the small, ordinary things that keep a mortal body alive, even as that body is slowly becoming something that will not need any of them.

She asks me questions. What is my favourite colour. What food do I miss. What is my earliest memory. She writes nothing down, but I can see her filing the answers away, building a map of who I was before the threshold. She calls it a trail of breadcrumbs. A way for me to find my way back.

I do not know if it will work. The ascension will take my body. It will take my ability to touch the physical world. It may take parts of me that I do not yet know I am losing. But I do not think it will take my memories. The Firstborn said that the Threshold-born who came before me remembered their mortal lives. Alyra remembered. And if Alyra remembered, I will remember.
