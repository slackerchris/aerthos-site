I told Anya tonight what I have been afraid to tell myself.

She did not flinch. She did not look away. She made a joke about knife work and emotional support using the same skills, and she took my hand, and she sat with me in the dark while I said the words I had been holding behind my teeth for weeks. I am going to lose my body. I am going to become something vast and luminous and alone. And she said she would be terrified with me.

I have been shaped by Lyssara my entire life. I have felt the Veilweaver's presence in the pattern, a cool silver thread running through everything I have ever done. I have been guided, moulded, prepared for something I did not understand. And now Nyth'aresh presses against the other side of my mind, showing me the truths Lyssara's order was built to conceal. Two goddesses, fighting for what I will believe. And I am standing in the middle, trying to hold both.

Anya does not care about the goddesses. She cares about me. She asks what I want, not what the divine requires. She asked me that tonight, and I did not have an answer. I have been so focused on what I am becoming that I have forgotten to ask what I want to become.

I want to stay with her. With Bjorn. With the family I have found. I want to sit beside fires and eat terrible road food and argue about nothing. I want to be ordinary. I want to be mortal.

I cannot be mortal. The transformation will not let me. But I can choose what the transformation means. I can choose to carry my memories with me, intact, through the threshold. I can choose to remember.

I am writing this so that I remember. When the power is burning through me and the primal layer is pulling me under, I want to look back at these words and know that I was afraid, and that I was loved, and that I chose anyway.
