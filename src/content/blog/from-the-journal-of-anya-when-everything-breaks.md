Three days away changed everything.

The Crows found me. Professionals, not alley blades. They let me walk away because the road home was worth more than my throat.

Came back to the grove dying. Not spellwork, Elara says. Not the Weave. Pressure came through the wound where her magic used to be and nearly killed Lianor.

So we go south, back through Silverford for supplies, then into the Whispering Woods because old books promise a kind of wild magic that transforms instead of destroys. That is the hopeful version. I do not trust hopeful versions.

Lianor is coming. Too old for the journey, by his own accounting, which means he has started complaining early to save time. He is also the only person alive who can argue with half the books we need, so that settles that.

Never thought I'd miss the certainty of a blade in an alley. At least then you knew what was trying to kill you. Now we're walking into a forest that might not kill us at all, just turn us into people who wish it had.

Elara thinks transformation is hope. I think hope is the most dangerous thing we could carry into those woods.
