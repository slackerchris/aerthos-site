<div class="journal-note">*An in-world journal entry from Anya, one of the three protagonists of the Age of Reclamation trilogy.*</div>

---

Three years. That's what we had. Three years of watching each other's backs, sharing bad ale and worse jokes, pretending we were just three fools stumbling through a broken world. Turns out we were family, and I was too stupid to see it until the mountain came calling.

Bjorn's gone south and west to his king and his duty and his brother who might be dead by now. Left us with gifts and promises and that look in his eyes like he was memorizing our faces for a tomb. Stone holds, he says. We'll see about that.

The dwarf thinks he owes them something. Three years of exile, and he still bleeds for the bastards who threw him out for saving kin. That's the difference between us. I know what loyalty costs. He still thinks it's free.

Elara says the threads are gone. Says the gods are silent now, and maybe they are. But I saw her face when the grove went quiet after Bjorn left, saw her touch those pale scars like a buried nerve had stirred. If whatever burned her hollow is waking again, I'll be there when it does. Someone has to keep her breathing when the dark decides it wants her back.

Two roads south. One north. Debts everywhere, and the ledger's written in blood.

The mountain can have him for now. But if those stone halls break his bones, I'll dig him out myself. That's a promise worth keeping.
