<div class="journal-note">*An in-world journal entry from Bjorn, son of Halvor, one of the three protagonists of the Age of Reclamation trilogy.*</div>

---

Roads remember more honestly than halls.

A hall remembers what was declared there. Which oath was spoken. Which name was struck from the stone. A hall keeps the shape of judgment and calls it truth.

A road remembers weight.

Boots. Blood. Weather. The silence after a choice has been made. The sound of someone walking beside you when they have no debt requiring it.

I heard my grandfather's hand in the axe today.

Not his voice. A voice can be changed by memory. A hammer-stroke cannot. The pause before the second rune, the turn he favored where another smith would have cut deeper, the old patience in the steel. The stone woke it, or taught it, or reminded it. I do not yet know the right word.

I know only that exile did not take it.

This should comfort me.

It does not.

Anya speaks of weakness as if it were a knife someone else put in her hand. I have seen her choose where not to cut.

Elara marks cracks before others know there is a wall. She does not test the foundation beneath herself.

Both of them are still walking beside me.

We go north now.

The road has taken our measure. It will ask for more.
